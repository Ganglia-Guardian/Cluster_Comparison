================================================================================
 CLUSTERING POSES UNDER KNOWN IMU CLUSTERS
 Methods, rationale, and glossary
 Scope: week-8 sessions wk8lc (subject 2lc) and wk8mp (subject 2mp)
 Status: 2-session pilot. Designed to scale to a week-by-week analysis later.
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
0. PURPOSE & SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We have two parallel descriptions of the same recording session:

  (1) IMU/func clustering  -- a pre-existing behavioral codebook built from
      head-mounted inertial (accelerometer/gyro) data. Each ~0.345 s "window"
      already carries a discrete IMU cluster label (called ClusterIdx, or the
      "func" label). We treat these as GIVEN / KNOWN ("the prior").

  (2) Pose / internal geometry -- derived here from 3D keypoint tracking.

This document covers ONE direction of analysis: taking the KNOWN IMU clusters as
a prior and asking how the POSE data is organized underneath them -- i.e.
"clustering poses under known IMU clusters." (The reverse direction, clustering
IMU data under pose priors with Affinity Propagation, exists in the codebase but
is out of scope here; see kp_pose_prior_imu.py.)

Central question: when you already know an animal's IMU-defined behavior, does
its body geometry (posture) carry coherent, non-random structure? And does that
structure relate to the IMU codebook's own internal structure?

Terms in ALL-CAPS or "quoted" on first use are defined inline and again in the
Appendix glossary (Section 11).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. DATA SOURCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Per session there are two files:

  kp_analysis/data/<session>/multicam_3d_results.csv
      EKS ("Ensemble Kalman Smoother") 3D keypoint output, in Lightning-Pose /
      DeepLabCut multi-camera format. EKS is a smoother that fuses multiple
      camera views into a single 3D estimate per keypoint, plus a per-axis
      POSTERIOR VARIANCE (its uncertainty). The file has a 3-row header:
          row 1 scorer     (all "ensemble-kalman_tracker")
          row 2 bodyparts  (keypoint name, repeated per coordinate)
          row 3 coords     (x, y, z, x_posterior_var, y_posterior_var,
                            z_posterior_var)
      17 keypoints x 6 columns; 108,000 frames at 30 fps (~60 min).
      The data is FRAME-UNANCHORED: there is no global / gravitational x-y-z
      reference, so absolute coordinates are meaningless and differ wildly
      between sessions (see Section 4).

  data/<imu_subject>/Cluster_detail_results.csv
      One row per IMU window across ALL weeks of that subject:
          ClusterIdx   -- the IMU (func) cluster label
          Timestamp    -- start time on a continuous global IMU clock (seconds)
          Folder_Name  -- which week / recording the window belongs to
      The IMU window-by-window similarity matrix lives separately in
      data/<imu_subject>/session_1_out.mat (see Section 7.3).

  Session -> subject mapping (IMPORTANT, verified by hand):
      wk8lc  <->  2lc   (week folder "w8")
      wk8mp  <->  2mp   (week folder "week8")

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. KEYPOINT SELECTION  (which, and why)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The full marker set has 17 keypoints:
    snout, ear_left, ear_right, head, back_top, back_mid, back_base,
    tail_mid, tail_end, paw_front_left, ankle_front_left, paw_front_right,
    ankle_front_right, paw_rear_left, ankle_rear_left, paw_rear_right,
    ankle_rear_right

We dropped TWO keypoints -- paw_front_left and back_base -- because they are
100% missing in wk8mp. Any pairwise distance touching a missing keypoint is
unusable, and we require a COMMON keypoint set so the two sessions share an
identical feature space (and so future cross-week comparisons are commensurate).

  => 15 "common keypoints" -> C(15,2) = 105 pairwise distances per frame.

Why pairwise distances at all? Because the data is frame-unanchored. The
PAIRWISE DISTANCE MATRIX between keypoints is invariant to translation and
rotation of the whole animal -- it captures only INTERNAL GEOMETRY (the shape of
the skeleton), which is exactly what is comparable across sessions recorded in
different camera frames. (Distances are also reflection-invariant, i.e. they
cannot distinguish a left turn from its mirror-image right turn; we accepted this
for posture work.)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. DATA CLEANING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Missing data / interpolation
    wk8lc keypoints are complete (0% NaN). wk8mp has ~12% scattered missing
    values (beyond the two fully-absent keypoints). We linearly INTERPOLATE each
    coordinate over time (per keypoint, per axis); the series ends are held flat.
    After interpolation, 0 residual NaNs remain.
    [kp_features.load_keypoints / _interp_gaps]

3.2 Posterior variance
    EKS provides per-coordinate posterior variance (localization uncertainty).
    It is available for confidence-weighting or masking of unreliable distances;
    in the current pipeline we rely on interpolation and do not down-weight,
    but the variances are loaded and preserved for future use.

3.3 Scale normalization
    The two sessions live in completely different coordinate frames -- the median
    pairwise distance is ~91 (wk8lc) vs ~1252 (wk8mp), a ~14x difference from
    camera setup. To make sessions comparable WITHOUT destroying within-session
    posture variation, we divide every distance in a session by that session's
    single MEDIAN pairwise distance (one scalar per session). This removes fixed
    body-size / camera-depth scale but preserves relative posture changes. (A
    per-frame normalization would have partly erased real posture variation, so
    we deliberately used a session-level scalar instead.)
    [kp_features.normalize_scale]

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. FEATURE CONSTRUCTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Per frame:
  POSTURE features  = the 105 scale-normalized pairwise 3D Euclidean distances.
                      This is the "internal geometry" of that frame.
  MOTION features   = the time-derivative of those 105 distances, i.e. the
                      DEFORMATION RATE (how fast the internal geometry is
                      changing). Computed with a Savitzky-Golay derivative
                      (window length 7 frames, polynomial order 2), which
                      smooths and differentiates in one pass to avoid the noise
                      amplification of a raw frame-to-frame difference. Scaled to
                      units/second using fps = 30.
                      NOTE: this is an INTERNAL-frame deformation rate, NOT
                      locomotor speed -- true speed needs a global frame, which
                      the unanchored data lacks. The head IMU, by contrast,
                      measures GLOBAL-frame dynamics, so IMU "motion" and pose
                      "deformation rate" are different quantities by construction.
  [kp_features.pairwise_distances / motion_features]

For the IMU-prior->pose analyses the POSTURE features are primary; MOTION is used
mainly to define "transitional" vs "sustained" frames (Section 8.0).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. TEMPORAL RECONCILIATION  (how the two clocks were aligned)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The keypoint video and the IMU recording run on different clocks and durations:

      keypoint video : 30 fps, full ~60 min, 108,000 frames
      IMU week-8      : ~5,964 (2lc) / ~5,977 (2mp) windows of ~0.345 s each,
                        ~35 min total, ~170 Hz raw IMU (~60 IMU samples/window)

Reconciliation assumptions (confirmed for this rig):
  * SHARED START TRIGGER: the camera and IMU began together, so IMU window 0
    corresponds to video frame 0.
  * The IMU therefore covers only the FIRST ~35 min of the 60-min video
    (~63,000 of the 108,000 frames overlap; later frames have no IMU label).

Procedure [kp_features.load_func_windows / assign_frames_to_windows /
           aggregate_to_windows; orchestrated in kp_cluster_compare.build_windows]:
  1. For the session's week folder, take the IMU windows, sort by Timestamp,
     and subtract the first Timestamp so window start times are seconds-relative
     to the shared origin (frame 0).
  2. Each video frame f is placed at time t = f / 30 s and assigned to the IMU
     window whose [start, end) interval contains t. Window end = next window's
     start; the last window gets the median window duration.
  3. Frames past the IMU end, or falling in one of the ~6 small inter-window
     gaps, are dropped (label -1).
  4. Per-frame POSTURE features are averaged within each window (mean); MOTION
     features are aggregated as mean-of-absolute-value (motion magnitude).
     Result: ~10.6 frames per window, one feature vector per IMU window, aligned
     1:1 with that window's IMU ClusterIdx.

Independent sanity check: the number of frames landing inside the IMU span
(~63,080) matches the 30 fps x 2,102 s prediction (~63,060) to within 0.03%,
corroborating the shared-start + constant-30-fps model.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. CLUSTERING ALGORITHMS USED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 Pose clustering (the thing we build)
    Pipeline: StandardScaler (z-score each of the 105 features)
              -> PCA retaining 95% of variance (decorrelates the highly
                 redundant distance features and reduces dimensionality)
              -> KMeans, k = 30 clusters, n_init = 10, fixed random seed.
    Output: a "pose cluster" label per window. k = 30 was chosen as a moderate
    granularity; the qualitative results were checked to be stable across k.
    [kp_cluster_compare.embed / cluster]

6.2 IMU clusters (the KNOWN prior)
    Not re-computed here -- these are the pre-existing func codebook from the IMU
    pipeline: 111 (2lc) / 91 (2mp) distinct clusters present in week 8. They are
    a fine VECTOR QUANTIZATION of the IMU feature stream.

6.3 Sub-clustering within an IMU cluster (Section 8.2)
    HDBSCAN (density-based; labels sparse points as "noise" = -1) on the pose
    embedding of one IMU cluster's windows. min_cluster_size = max(5, 10% of the
    IMU cluster's window count). Chosen because we expected, and wanted to detect,
    "a few dense pose modes + noise" rather than a forced fixed k.

6.4 Hierarchical grouping of pose clusters (Section 8.3)
    Average-linkage agglomerative clustering on a pose-cluster x pose-cluster
    distance (1 - co-recruitment correlation), visualized as a DENDROGRAM.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. THE IMU SIMILARITY MATRIX  (used in Sections 8.4)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 Location: data/<subject>/session_1_out.mat (MATLAB v7.3 / HDF5), dataset
    /Clusters/sim. It is an ALL-WEEKS window x window IMU similarity:
    113,627 x 113,627 (2lc) / 113,597 x 113,597 (2mp).
7.2 Scale: values <= 0, self-similarity (diagonal) = 0, more-negative = less
    similar (a negative-distance-style similarity). The "minimum value in the
    matrix" (~ -0.34) is the conventional Affinity-Propagation preference.
7.3 Alignment: /Clusters/idx equals the Cluster_detail_results.csv ClusterIdx
    column in order (verified), so sim rows correspond row-for-row to detail
    rows. We extract the week-8 block in the SAME Timestamp-sorted order used by
    build_windows, then symmetrize. A direct check confirms the extracted block's
    per-window IMU labels equal the analysis windows' labels exactly.
    [kp_pose_prior_imu.extract_sim_from_mat]

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. STEP-BY-STEP METHOD: CLUSTERING POSES UNDER KNOWN IMU CLUSTERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
All steps operate on the per-window POSTURE features and the per-window IMU
ClusterIdx, aligned as in Section 5.

8.0  Define transitional vs sustained windows  [kp_silhouette.py]
     Pose space is continuous, so windows captured mid-movement sit between
     cluster cores and blur boundaries. We quantify this:
       - SILHOUETTE score per window (how well it fits its pose cluster vs the
         next-nearest; range -1..+1).
       - "Transitional-ness" = the window's MOTION magnitude (mean |deformation
         rate|).
     Finding: silhouette falls strongly as transitional-ness rises
     (Spearman -0.51 lc / -0.63 mp), confirming that transitional windows are the
     boundary-blurring ones. Some k-means clusters are really "transition bins"
     (high motion, ~0 silhouette) rather than true sustained postures.

8.1  Aligned subset: which IMU clusters map cleanly to a pose?
     [kp_aligned_clusters.py]
     Build the IMU x pose contingency table. For each IMU cluster, find its
     best-matching pose cluster and score the match with:
       PURITY  = P(pose = best | IMU)              (concentration)
       LIFT    = purity / P(pose = best)           (enrichment over chance)
       JACCARD = overlap / union of the two sets   (one-to-one-ness)
       p       = HYPERGEOMETRIC survival probability (chance of overlap this big)
     Multiple-testing controlled by Bonferroni across IMU clusters.
     Finding: most IMU clusters have no posture signature, BUT a real minority do
     -- the rest/low-motion mode maps onto the dominant resting posture at
     65-79% purity (p ~ 1e-33..1e-39), and a handful of specific behaviors are
     enriched 5-12x into interpretable postures (forelimb spread, body
     elongation, snout extension, head-turn).

8.2  Conditional pose modes: sub-cluster poses under each IMU cluster
     [kp_conditional_pose.py]
     For each IMU cluster with >= 40 windows, run HDBSCAN on its windows' pose
     embedding. Report number of pose MODES, the share of windows in the top
     mode(s), and the NOISE fraction.
     Finding: most IMU behaviors resolve into a few dominant pose modes + noise
     (50/60 lc and 35/53 mp are "clean": few modes, >=40% covered, <=60% noise;
     median 2-3 modes). I.e. a given IMU behavior is realized by a small set of
     postures, not an unstructured smear.

8.3  Pose grouping: do the recruited poses co-occur beyond chance?
     [kp_pose_grouping.py]
     - Global test: chi-square on the IMU x pose table, with CRAMER'S V as a
       sample-size-normalized effect size.
     - CO-RECRUITMENT: describe each pose cluster by its column of STANDARDIZED
       PEARSON RESIDUALS over IMU clusters (how enriched/depleted it is in each
       IMU behavior vs independence). Two pose clusters "co-recruit" when these
       residual profiles correlate -- they are favored by the same IMU behaviors.
       A PERMUTATION null (shuffle pose labels, keep IMU fixed) gives a per-pair
       z-score; pairs with z > 2.58 are co-recruited beyond chance (p < 0.01).
     - Group the pose clusters by 1 - profile-correlation (average linkage) and
       draw the DENDROGRAM ("pose super-groups").
     - Cross-reference: a MANTEL test (rank correlation of two distance matrices)
       between co-recruitment distance and pose-centroid GEOMETRIC distance.
     Findings: association is highly significant but modest (Cramer's V ~ 0.19);
     27 (lc) / 39 (mp) pose pairs co-recruit beyond chance (vs ~2 expected),
     forming coherent posture families. CRUCIALLY the Mantel vs geometry is ~0 --
     the IMU-driven grouping of poses is NOT just "geometrically similar poses
     cluster together"; it is a genuine cross-modal axis.

8.4  Cross-modal coherence: does IMU similarity predict pose recruitment?
     [kp_imu_pose_coherence.py]   (KEY RESULT)
     Represent each IMU cluster two ways:
       - POSE PROFILE = P(pose | IMU), its distribution over the 30 pose clusters
         (its "pose mode set").
       - IMU-space position via the similarity matrix (Section 7), aggregated to
         an IMU-cluster x IMU-cluster MEAN-SIMILARITY matrix.
     MANTEL test (permutation): IMU-space distance vs pose-profile distance
     (JENSEN-SHANNON divergence between the P(pose|IMU) distributions).
       - Positive => IMU-similar behaviors recruit similar postures (COHERENT).
       - ~0      => pose recruitment is independent of IMU similarity.
     Findings: STRONG coherence -- Spearman +0.60 (lc) / +0.70 (mp), p ~ 5e-4.
     This holds even after removing the REST FAMILY (rest-dominated IMU clusters
     and the large low-motion "rest" pose clusters): wk8lc stays at +0.39 on 40
     active clusters (p ~ 5e-4). (wk8mp's no-rest cell is directionally +0.85 but
     rests on only 8 clusters -- underpowered, because in mp ~52/60 IMU clusters
     are rest-dominated.)
     Interpretation: the cross-modal signal lives in RELATIONAL STRUCTURE, not in
     coincident hard labels. Window-by-window agreement is weak (AMI ~ 0.05; see
     8.5), yet the GEOMETRY of the IMU-cluster space maps onto the geometry of the
     pose-recruitment space. The IMU->posture map is coherent but non-trivial: it
     preserves IMU relational structure while reorganizing pose space along an
     axis that is not raw posture shape.

8.5  Window-level baseline (for contrast)  [kp_cluster_compare.py]
     ADJUSTED MUTUAL INFORMATION (AMI) between the pose-cluster labels and the
     IMU ClusterIdx labels, window by window. AMI ~ 0.05 (both sessions), barely
     above a label-shuffle floor and flat across k = 30..120 -- so the two
     HARD PARTITIONS do not coincide. The contrast between this weak AMI and the
     strong relational coherence in 8.4 is the headline of the whole analysis.

8.6  Rarefaction: is the grouping structure robust / saturated?
     [kp_pose_grouping_rarefaction.py]
     Subsample windows (pose & IMU labels fixed) at increasing depths; at each
     depth recompute Cramer's V and the count of beyond-chance co-recruited pairs,
     with confidence bands over repeated draws.
     Findings:
       - Cramer's V converges to 0.187 in BOTH sessions; small-sample estimates
         are biased HIGH (~0.47 at 500 windows). So V ~ 0.19 is robust, but a V
         from a few hundred windows would overstate the effect ~2.5x.
       - The co-recruited-pair COUNT is NOT saturated -- still rising at full N --
         so 27/39 are LOWER BOUNDS; the grouping is power-limited.
       - At every matched depth, mp shows more grouping than lc, so mp's richer
         structure is real, not a sampling artifact.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. CURRENT CONCLUSION  (2-session pilot)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the scope "clustering poses under known IMU clusters":

  * Hard window-by-window agreement between IMU behavior and posture is weak
    (AMI ~ 0.05).
  * Yet the modalities are strongly coherent at the RELATIONAL level: IMU
    behaviors that are similar in IMU space recruit similar posture distributions
    (Mantel +0.60..+0.70; +0.39 among active, non-rest behaviors).
  * Most individual IMU behaviors resolve into a few characteristic posture modes
    (HDBSCAN), and the recruited poses co-occur in coherent families beyond chance
    -- but along a cross-modal axis that is NOT reducible to posture geometry.
  * Effect size (Cramer's V ~ 0.19) is modest but robustly estimated; the number
    of detectable co-recruitment relationships is currently power-limited.

In one line: knowing the IMU behavior tells you a specific, coherent thing about
posture distribution, even though no single posture pins down the exact IMU
codeword.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. LIMITATIONS & FUTURE WORK (week-by-week)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  * Only 2 sessions, single week (week 8). The co-recruitment pair counts are not
    saturated (Section 8.6), so more data should reveal more structure.
  * The "rest family" inflates several statistics; it has been controlled for but
    in wk8mp this leaves few active clusters -- more data would fix the power.
  * 30 fps and the shared-start assumption are taken as exact; no per-frame sync
    signal is used. A drift of ~0.1% fps would slip the tail of the alignment by
    a few windows.
  * FUTURE: with enough sessions to run a WEEK-BY-WEEK analysis, we expect much
    sharper results. The IMU pipeline already tracks behavior across weeks
    (disease progression, drug conditions); adding the geometric/posture modality
    means each behavioral state can be characterized by BOTH its motion signature
    AND its posture signature over time. The relational-coherence framework here
    (Mantel of IMU-space vs pose-recruitment) is the natural statistic to track
    longitudinally, and rarefaction provides the fair cross-week comparison at
    equal sampling depth.

12. APPENDIX -- GLOSSARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EKS (Ensemble Kalman Smoother)
    The pose-estimation smoother that produced the 3D keypoints; fuses multiple
    camera views and reports per-axis posterior variance.

IMU / func cluster / ClusterIdx / "the prior" / codebook
    The pre-existing behavioral clustering from head-mounted inertial data. Each
    window has one discrete IMU cluster label (ClusterIdx, a.k.a. the "func"
    label). 111 (2lc) / 91 (2mp) clusters in week 8. Treated as KNOWN input.

Window
    One IMU clustering unit, ~0.345 s (~60 raw IMU samples, ~10.6 video frames).
    All analyses are at window resolution.

Frame-unanchored
    Keypoints have no global/gravitational reference frame; absolute coordinates
    are arbitrary and differ between sessions. Forces the use of internal geometry.

Internal geometry / pairwise distance matrix
    The set of distances between keypoints within a single frame. Invariant to
    translation and rotation (and reflection). 105 values from 15 keypoints.

Common keypoints
    The 15 keypoints present in BOTH sessions (the full 17 minus paw_front_left
    and back_base, which are absent in wk8mp).

Posterior variance
    EKS per-coordinate uncertainty. Available for confidence weighting; currently
    unused beyond being loaded.

Scale normalization
    Dividing all of a session's distances by that session's median distance, to
    remove fixed body-size/camera scale while preserving posture variation.

Posture features
    The 105 scale-normalized pairwise distances per frame/window -- a static
    description of body shape.

Motion features / deformation rate
    Time-derivative (Savitzky-Golay) of the posture features: how fast internal
    geometry changes. An INTERNAL-frame quantity, distinct from IMU global-frame
    dynamics and from locomotor speed.

Transitional vs sustained
    A window is "transitional" if its deformation rate is high (and silhouette
    low) -- caught mid-movement; "sustained" if low deformation / high silhouette
    -- a held posture.

Silhouette
    Per-item clustering-fit score in [-1, +1]: how much closer an item is to its
    own cluster than to the next-nearest. High = well-clustered core.

Shared start trigger
    The assumption (true for this rig) that camera and IMU began simultaneously,
    so video frame 0 = IMU window 0; the basis for temporal reconciliation.

Pose cluster / pose mode
    "Pose cluster": a KMeans cluster (k=30) of posture features. "Pose mode": a
    dense pose grouping found by HDBSCAN within a single IMU cluster's windows.

KMeans / PCA / StandardScaler
    The pose-clustering pipeline: z-score features, reduce to 95%-variance PCA
    components, partition into k=30 clusters.

HDBSCAN
    Density-based clustering that finds a variable number of dense clusters and
    labels sparse points as NOISE (-1). Used to find "a few pose modes + noise"
    under each IMU cluster.

Noise (fraction)
    Share of an IMU cluster's windows HDBSCAN leaves unassigned (label -1).

Vector quantization
    Representing a continuous signal by discrete cluster/codebook labels; what the
    IMU pipeline does to the inertial stream (hence "codebook").

Contingency table
    The IMU-cluster x pose-cluster matrix of window counts; the basis for purity,
    chi-square, residuals, and co-recruitment.

Purity / Lift / Jaccard
    For an IMU->pose match: purity = fraction of the IMU cluster in its best pose;
    lift = purity divided by that pose's overall frequency (enrichment over
    chance); Jaccard = overlap / union of the two window sets.

Hypergeometric test
    Probability that an IMU/pose overlap as large as observed would occur by
    chance given the two set sizes; yields the p-value for an alignment.

Bonferroni
    Conservative multiple-testing correction (multiply p by number of tests).

Chi-square / Cramer's V
    Chi-square tests whether IMU and pose are independent; Cramer's V rescales it
    to a 0-1 effect size (~0.19 here = weak-moderate, robustly estimated).

Standardized Pearson residual
    (observed - expected)/sqrt(expected) for a contingency cell; how enriched or
    depleted a pose is in an IMU behavior relative to independence. The per-pose
    vector of these over IMU clusters is its "co-recruitment profile."

Co-recruitment
    Two pose clusters co-recruit when they are enriched in the SAME IMU behaviors
    (their residual profiles correlate). Tested for significance by permutation.

Permutation test
    Significance by repeatedly shuffling labels to build a null distribution, then
    locating the real statistic within it. Used for co-recruitment z-scores and
    for Mantel p-values.

Dendrogram / super-group
    Tree from hierarchical clustering of the pose clusters by co-recruitment
    distance; cutting it yields pose "super-groups" (posture families).

Mantel test
    Rank correlation between two distance matrices over the same items, with a
    permutation p-value. Used twice: (a) co-recruitment vs pose geometry (~0), and
    (b) IMU-space distance vs pose-recruitment distance (+0.60..+0.70, the key
    result).

Pose profile / pose mode set
    P(pose | IMU): an IMU cluster's distribution over the 30 pose clusters -- the
    set of postures it recruits, and how often.

Jensen-Shannon divergence/distance
    A symmetric, bounded measure of how different two probability distributions
    are; used to compare two IMU clusters' pose profiles.

Mean-similarity matrix
    IMU-cluster x IMU-cluster matrix whose entries are the average window-level
    IMU similarity between the two clusters' windows; the IMU-space geometry.

Relational structure / coherence
    Agreement at the level of BETWEEN-CLUSTER relationships (which behaviors are
    near which) rather than coincident hard labels. The central finding: IMU and
    pose are relationally coherent (Mantel +0.6..+0.7) despite weak label-level
    agreement (AMI ~0.05).

Rest family
    The low-motion "rest" pose cluster(s) (large + bottom-quartile deformation)
    and the IMU clusters dominated by them. A known skew; excluded as a robustness
    check (kp_imu_pose_coherence.py --exclude-rest).

AMI / NMI (Adjusted / Normalized Mutual Information)
    Information shared between two label sets, window by window. AMI is chance-
    adjusted, so comparable across differing cluster counts. ~0.05 here.

Rarefaction
    Subsampling to a common depth (repeatedly) to remove sample-size bias and test
    whether a statistic has saturated. Shows Cramer's V converges (~0.19) while the
    co-recruited-pair count does not (a lower bound).
